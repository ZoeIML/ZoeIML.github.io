Which timebox and habits techniques did you try?

I tried the Pomodoro technique for a writing task, as well as trying the Fogg Method to introduce new habits around self-care. It's early days with the latter yet. I already use mindfulness / meditation during the day.

What did you find interesting?

For me, the most interesting point was the idea that behaviour should come before motivation before motivation is not enough to estabish a new habit. I liked the idea of getting back to basics and starting small, building up to a habit and letting go of the "thinking big" which is something that is easy to focus on instead.

What worked for you?

I liked the Pomodoro technique for the task I was completing. It's a little early to tell about the habits but so far, so good. Timeboxing is very similar to a different technique one of my workplaces had a few years ago, and it was pretty effective for me then.

Which will you experiment further with?

I think I need to continue with developing the tiny habits and also to practiec the Pomodoro technique more because I found it pretty challenging to concentrate for 25 minutes straight!
